How to Calculate GST in India (Complete Guide 2026)

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect, comprehensive, multi-stage, destination-based tax levied on every value addition in India. Whether you are a small business owner, a freelancer, or a consumer, understanding how to compute your GST liabilities accurately is crucial. Our online GST calculator simplifies this entire process for you instantly.

GST Calculation Formulas

If you wish to calculate GST manually without a software tool, here are the exact mathematical formulas used by Indian accountants:

1. Formula to Add GST (Exclusive GST)

  • GST Amount = (Original Cost x GST Rate) / 100
  • Net Price = Original Cost + GST Amount

Example: Adding 18% GST to ₹10,000.
GST = (10,000 x 18) / 100 = ₹1,800. Final Price = ₹11,800.

2. Formula to Remove GST (Reverse / Inclusive GST)

  • Base Amount = Final Price / (1 + (GST Rate / 100))
  • GST Extracted = Final Price - Base Amount

Example: Extracting 18% GST from ₹11,800.
Base = 11,800 / (1 + 0.18) = ₹10,000. GST = ₹1,800.

Understanding CGST, SGST, and IGST

India uses a dual GST model, meaning taxation is administered by both the Central and State Governments. Our calculator automatically splits the tax based on your selection:

  • Intra-State Sales (Within the same state): The GST is divided equally into Central GST (CGST) and State GST (SGST). Example: An 18% tax becomes 9% CGST and 9% SGST.
  • Inter-State Sales (Between two different states): Integrated GST (IGST) is applied, which is collected by the Central Government. Example: The full 18% is applied as IGST.

Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

What are the new GST rates in 2026?

GST 2.0 (effective September 2025) simplified slabs to 0%, 5%, 18%, and 40%. The 12% and 28% slabs have been largely removed. Most essentials are now at 5%, standard goods at 18%, and luxury/sin goods at 40%.

What is a Reverse GST Calculator?

A reverse GST calculator (or inclusive calculator) works backward. If you have a total invoice amount of ₹1,180 (which includes 18% GST), the reverse calculator extracts the tax (₹180) to show you the original base price (₹1,000).

What items are at 0% GST?

Essential items like fresh fruits, vegetables, milk, eggs, unbranded dairy products, fresh bread, educational materials, and 33 lifesaving drugs are now at 0% GST under GST 2.0.

What is the 40% GST rate?

The 40% rate applies to luxury and "sin" goods like premium cars, aerated beverages, tobacco products, online gaming, betting, and casinos. This replaced the old 28% + compensation cess structure.
